What are food deserts?



Working definition:

“The term ‘food desert,’ originally coined in the United Kingdom, describes low-income neighborhoods, both urban and rural, that have limited access to full-service supermarkets or grocery stores. Because supermarkets generally offer a variety of healthy foods at reasonable cost, food access is defined by proximity to a supermarket or large grocery store.” 1
